Landau levels and its degeneracy in different gauges

Discuss Landau levels in symmetric and Landau gauge

周三给本科生讲量子力学习题课的时候，讲到了朗道能级。课上我是用朗道规范来推导的。用这个规范推导比较简单一些，因为很容易的就能化成一个谐振子方程，学生们都比较熟悉。我在这篇博客里面对它做个总结，再用对称规范做一个类似的推导，然后再讨论下朗道能级简并度。如果有时间的话这些材料可以在习题课上再讲讲，让他们具体感受下规范的选取的任意性。

我们考虑的问题是一个带电为\(q\)质量为\(m\)的电荷，在含有匀强磁场\(B_0\hat z\)的无限大空间的运动。电磁场中的哈密顿量写作：\[H=\frac{1}{2m}(-i\hbar\nabla-q\vec A)^2+q\varphi\]

朗道规范

能级

朗道规范选取\(\vec A=-B_0y\hat x\), \(\varphi=0\)。这样，哈密顿量便写成： \[ H=\frac{1}{2m}\left[(\hat p_x+qB_0y)^2+\hat p_y ^2+\hat p_z^2\right] \] 显然，\({\hat p_x,\hat p_z,H}\)相互对易，我们可以选取它们的共同本征波函数作为定态薛定谔方程的解: \[ \psi(x,y,z)=e^{i(p_xx/\hbar+p_zz/\hbar)}\chi(y) \]

带入薛定谔方程之中，得到： \[ \frac{1}{2m}\hat p_y^2\chi(y)+\frac{1}{2}m\omega^2(y-y_0)^2\chi(y)=(E-\frac{p_z^2}{2m})\chi(y) \]

其中，\(\omega=qB_0/m\)，\(y_0=-p_x/qB_0\).

上面的方程是我们熟知的谐振子，所以立即可以得到：\(E_n=(n+1/2)\hbar w+p_z^2/2m\)，\(\chi_n(y)=\bar\chi_n(y,y_0)\)。

能级的简并度

由于能级\(E_n\)不依赖于\(y_0=-p_x/qB_0\),且对于无限大系统\(p_x\in(-\infty,\infty)\),因此朗道能级的简并度是无穷的。但如果是一个有限大的系统, \(S=l_xl_y\)，选取周期性边界条件，则要求\(p_xl_x/\hbar=2\pi n_x\)，对应的\(y_0\)的选取也相应的量子化：\(y_0=-n_x h/qB_0l_x\)，因而在有限大的\(l_y\)范围内，\(y_0\)可取值的数目为：\(\Phi/\Phi_0\)，其中\(\Phi=B_0S\)为穿过整个区域的磁通，而\(\Phi_0=h/q\)为一个flux quanta.

对称规范

能级

对称规范选取\(\vec A=B_0(x\hat y-y\hat x)/2\), \(\varphi=0\)。这样 \[ \begin{aligned} H&=\frac{1}{2m}(\hat p-q\vec A)^2\\ &=\frac{1}{2m}[\hat p^2+q^2A^2-q(\hat p\cdot \vec A+\vec A\cdot \hat p)]\\ &=\frac{1}{2m}[\hat p^2+q^2A^2-q(2\vec A\cdot \hat p-i\hbar\mathrm{div}\vec A)]\\ &=\frac{1}{2m}\hat p^2+\frac{1}{2}m\Omega^2(x^2+y^2)-\Omega\hat l_z \end{aligned} \]

其中，\(\Omega=\omega/2\)。显然，\(\hat p_z,H,\hat l_z\)互相对易，我们可以选取它们的共同本征态作为波函数(柱坐标下)： \[ \psi(r,\phi,z)=e^{i \bar m\phi}e^{ip_zz/\hbar}R(r) \]

柱坐标下哈密顿量可以化为： \[ H=-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}(\frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial}{\partial r}r\frac{\partial}{\partial r}+\frac{1}{r^2}\frac{\partial ^2}{\partial \phi^2}+\frac{\partial ^2}{\partial z^2})+\frac{1}{2}m\Omega^2r^2+i\Omega\hbar \frac{\partial}{\partial \phi} \]

定态薛定谔方程化为：

\[ \left[-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}(\frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial}{\partial r}r\frac{\partial}{\partial r}-\frac{\bar m^2}{r^2})+\frac{1}{2}m\Omega^2r^2\right]R(r)=(E-\frac{p_z^2}{2m}+\bar m \hbar\Omega)R(r)=E_rR(r) \]

令\(R(r)=r^{-\frac{1}{2}}u(r)\)，上式化为： \[\tag{1} -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\left( \frac{\partial^2}{\partial r^2}+\frac{\frac{1}{4}-\bar m^2}{r^2}\right)u(r)+\frac{1}{2}m\Omega^2r^2u(r)=E_ru(r) \]

This is nothing but the radial equation of \(3D\) harmonic oscillator, with the substitution of \(l(l+1)\rightarrow\bar m^2-1/4\), so: \[\tag{2} E_r=(j_{max}+l+3/2)\hbar \Omega=(j_{max}+|\bar m|+1)\hbar \Omega \] where \(j_{max}\) is even integer\(^1\).

At last we get: \[E=(j_{max}+|\bar m|+\bar m+1)\hbar \Omega+\frac{p_z^2}{2m}=(n+1/2)\hbar\omega+\frac{p_z^2}{2m}\].

能级的简并度

You can see for an arbitrary \(n\), there are infinite number of \(\bar m\), so the degeneracy of Laudau level is again infinite. How about finite size? Well, actually the \(l_z\) is bounded because \(r\) and \(p\) are bounded. Suppose we are considering a disk with radius \(R\),

  • The maximum \(l_z^{max}=qB_0R^2\), \(\bar m_{max}=2\Phi/\Phi_0\), why the degeneracy is doubled? They should independent of gauge choice.
  • Can \(\bar m\) be both signs? Since in classical mechanics, \(l_z\) can choose only one sign depending on the charge is negative or positive. However in our derivation, no restriction does \(\bar m\) has.

How to resolve these two paradox?

The first paradox

The problem is that we tangle the “Canonical angular momentum” with the physical one. Since \(\hat p_x,\hat p_y\) is canonical momentum, they are explicit gauge dependent, hence \(l_x\) is also gauge dependent.

The \(\hat l_z\) above admit no direct physical meaning, so the bounded condition for it is just invalid. The true physical momentum should be \[\tag{3} \begin{aligned} \hat L_z=&x\hat P_y-y\hat P_x\\ =&x(\hat p_y-qA_y)-y(\hat p_x-qA_x)\\ =&\hat l_z-\frac{qB}{2}r^2 \end{aligned} \]

Consider classically of left hand side, \(L_z=-qBr^2\), and\(^2\) \(l_z=-qBr^2\)so we can have \(|q|BR^2/2\hbar\) different values of \(\bar m\), this gives the right degeneracy: \(\Phi/\Phi_0\)

The second paradox

Equation(1) is the radial equation for a 3D harmonic oscillator with angular frequency \(\Omega\). Using viral theorem and refer to equation(2), we have\(^3\): \[ \frac{1}{2}m\Omega^2\langle r^2\rangle=\frac{1}{2}E_r\\ \langle r^2\rangle=\frac{\hbar}{m\Omega}(j_{max}+|\bar m|+1) \] With equation(3), we have \[ \langle L_z\rangle=\bar m\hbar-\frac{qB}{2m\Omega}(j_{max}+|\bar m|+1) \] If \(q>0\): \[ \langle L_z\rangle=-(j_{max}+1)\hbar-(|\bar m|-\bar m)\hbar \] always less than \(0\). If \(q<0\): \[ \langle L_z\rangle=(j_{max}+1)\hbar+(|\bar m|+\bar m)\hbar \] always larger than \(0\). In consistent with classical case.

\(^1:\) $j_{max}>0 $ and \(l=|\bar m|-1/2\) is chosen because even for the smallest nonzero \(|\bar m|=1\), if \(l=-|\bar m|-1/2=-3/2\). The asymptotic behavior of \(R(r)=r^{-\frac{1}{2}}u(r) \sim r^{-\frac{1}{2}}r^{l+1}=r^{-1}\) , then the integral \(\int R(r)^2 r \mathrm{d} r\) will be divergent near \(r=0\), i.e. the wave function is not normalizable.
