some operator identities and theorems

summary of some operator identities in physics

Here I will summarize some useful operator identities for future reference.

Exponential operator identities

Baker–Campbell–Hausdorff formula

\[e^ABe^{-A}=B+[A,B]+\frac{1}{2!}[A,[A,B]]+\cdots\] If \(C=[A,B]\) commutes with both \(A\) and \(B\). Then:

\[e^Ae^B=e^{A+B+\frac{1}{2}[A,B]}\]

Wilcox formula

In general, equation \(\frac{\partial}{\partial \lambda}e^{-H(\lambda)}\stackrel{?}{=} -e^{-H(\lambda)}\frac{\partial H}{\partial \lambda }\) is not true, the left hand side should follow this equation: \[ \frac{\partial}{\partial \lambda}e^{-\beta H}=-\int_0^\beta e^{-(\beta-u)H}\frac{\partial H}{\partial \lambda}e^{-uH}\mathrm{d}u \]

Above equation can lead to this equation: \[ [A,e^{-\beta H}]=-\int_0^\beta e^{-(\beta-u)H}[A,H]{\partial \lambda}e^{-uH}\mathrm{d}u \]
